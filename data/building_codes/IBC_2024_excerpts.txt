CHAPTER 16 — STRUCTURAL DESIGN

1604.3 Serviceability
  Structural systems and members thereof shall be designed to have adequate
  stiffness to limit deflections and lateral drift. Deflection limits are
  specified in Table 1604.3.

Table 1604.3 Deflection Limits:
  Roof members (live load): L/180
  Floor members (live load): L/360
  Exterior walls with brittle finishes: H/240
  Exterior walls with flexible finishes: H/120

1607.1 Live Loads
  See ASCE 7 for minimum live load requirements.

CHAPTER 17 — SPECIAL INSPECTIONS AND TESTS

1704.1 General
  Where special inspections are required by Section 1705, the owner shall
  employ an approved agency to perform inspections.

1705.3 Concrete Construction
  Continuous special inspection is required for:
  - Placement of concrete
  - Prestressed concrete
  - Erection of precast concrete

1705.3.1 Sampling fresh concrete and conducting slump/air content tests.
1705.3.2 Verification of concrete strength test results.

CHAPTER 19 — CONCRETE

1901.2 Plain and Reinforced Concrete
  Structural concrete shall be designed and constructed in accordance with ACI 318.

1905.1 Durability Requirements
  Concrete shall be designed for exposure categories as defined in ACI 318 Table 19.3.1.1.

Exposure Class:
  F0: No freezing/thawing
  F1: Moderate freezing/thawing  
  F2: Severe freezing/thawing
  F3: Severe freezing/thawing with deicing chemicals

  S0: No sulfate exposure
  S1: Moderate sulfate (150-1500 ppm)
  S2: Severe sulfate (1500-10000 ppm)
  S3: Very severe sulfate (>10000 ppm)

  W0: No water contact
  W1: In contact with water requiring low permeability

  C0: No corrosion protection required
  C1: Moderate corrosion protection (exposed to moisture)
  C2: Severe corrosion (deicing chemicals, brackish water, seawater)

CHAPTER 34 — EXISTING STRUCTURES

3401.1 Scope
  Provisions of this chapter shall control the repair, alteration, change
  of occupancy, addition, and relocation of existing buildings.

3404.1 Repairs
  Repairs to damaged elements shall use materials and methods that comply
  with the applicable requirements for new construction. The repaired
  structure shall not be less conforming than the existing structure.

3404.3 Substantial Structural Damage
  If a structure has sustained substantial structural damage, it shall be
  investigated by a registered design professional to determine if the
  structure can be repaired to provide the level of structural safety
  required for a new structure.
