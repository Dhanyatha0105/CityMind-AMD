ACI 318-19: Building Code Requirements for Structural Concrete

CHAPTER 24 — SERVICEABILITY REQUIREMENTS

Section 24.3 — Crack Control

24.3.2 Distribution of flexural reinforcement in one-way slabs and beams
  The maximum crack width shall not exceed 0.3mm for interior exposure conditions
  and 0.2mm for exterior exposure conditions.

24.3.2.1 The spacing of reinforcement closest to a tension face shall not exceed:
  s = 380(280/fs) - 2.5cc
  where fs is the calculated stress in reinforcement and cc is the clear cover.

24.3.3 Maximum permissible crack widths:
  - Interior dry conditions: 0.41mm
  - Interior humid conditions: 0.30mm
  - Exterior exposure: 0.25mm
  - Deicing chemical exposure: 0.18mm
  - Marine or splash zone: 0.15mm

CHAPTER 20 — STEEL REINFORCEMENT

Section 20.5.1.3 — Minimum Concrete Cover Requirements
  Concrete cast against and permanently in contact with ground: 75mm
  Concrete exposed to weather: 50mm for bars ≥20mm, 40mm for bars <20mm
  Concrete not exposed to weather: 40mm for slabs/walls, 40mm for beams/columns

20.5.1.3.4 Fire protection cover shall not be less than specified in Table 20.5.1.3.4.

CHAPTER 18 — EARTHQUAKE-RESISTANT STRUCTURES

Section 18.6.3 — Flexural Members of Special Moment Frames
  The positive moment strength at joint face shall not be less than 50% of the negative
  moment strength at that face.

18.7.5 — Minimum shear strength requirements for beams in seismic zones.

CHAPTER 22 — SECTIONAL STRENGTH

22.5 — One-Way Shear Strength
  Vc = 0.17λ√f'c × bw × d (simplified method)
  where λ is the lightweight concrete factor, f'c is compressive strength.

22.5.5.1 Minimum shear reinforcement:
  Av,min = 0.062√f'c × bw × s / fyt
  or 0.35 × bw × s / fyt (whichever is greater)

CHAPTER 25 — REINFORCEMENT DETAILS

25.4 — Development Length
  Development length for deformed bars in tension: ld = (fy × ψt × ψe × ψs × λ) / (1.1 × √f'c) × db

25.7.2 — Splice Requirements
  Lap splice length shall not be less than 1.0ld or 300mm.
